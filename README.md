# Trading Sentiment Analysis

Using Fear & Greed Index to Understand Market Behavior and Trade Performance

## Project Overview

This project explores how crypto market sentiment — measured by the Fear & Greed Index — influences trading behavior and profitability.
By combining historical trading data with corresponding sentiment classifications, we perform:

Data cleaning & preprocessing

Exploratory data analysis (EDA)

Statistical comparisons

Visualizations across multiple sentiment conditions

This project is part of a structured data analytics assignment.

## Key Questions Answered

How do profits vary across sentiment phases?

Do traders take larger positions during Fear or Greed cycles?

Which sentiment results in the most profitable trades?

How correlated are variables like trade size, fees, and PnL?

How does average daily profit change with sentiment over time?

## Summary of Insights
✔ Profit Distribution

Greed phases show high variance with occasional large profits.
Extreme Fear phases show tighter clusters around low or negative returns.

✔ Trading Volume

Trade size spikes during Fear and Greed, reflecting high-emotion market behavior.

✔ Correlations

Larger trades tend to produce higher PnL.
Fees correlate with trade size as expected.

✔ Daily Profit Trends

Sentiment cycles align with profit fluctuations.
Profits dipped sharply in early April 2024 during Extreme Greed before rebounding.

✔ Average Profit by Sentiment

Highest during Extreme Greed → Lowest during Extreme Fear.

✔ Average Trade Size

Traders commit more capital during Fear and Greed phases.
